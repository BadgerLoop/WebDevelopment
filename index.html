Preliminary Information

As of October 5th, we are 1 of 318 teams to advance to the Preliminary Design Briefing stage of the competition. We are currently planning for the Design Weekend in late January next year, at which we plan to secure funds sufficient for our BadgerLoop Competition Pod Prototype. In the following summer, we will travel to SpaceX’s headquarters and use their mile long test track to demonstrate our pod’s robust fabrication, mechanics, and controls. We hope to utilize the Design Weekend and Competition Weekend to show off some of our scalable and innovative Hyperloop subsystem technologies.

Revolutionizing Transportation since 2015

On June 15, 2015, our lives changed forever. SpaceX and Tesla announced a contest unlike any other, exciting technology enthusiasts across the planet and igniting the next wave of development of the next big thing in ground transportation. The competition: Bring Elon Musk's Hyperloop concept to life by designing and building a half scale pod. And keep everything open source knowledge.

What better way to develop this concept quickly and cheaply than unleash innovation hungry students and young professionals on it?

We'll be attending the Design Weekend at Texas A&M January 15 & 16, 2016 and testing out our physical model out in Hawthorne, CA on SpaceX's test track in June 2016.

BadgerLoop is a student organization created to represent UW-Madison in the SpaceX Hyperloop Pod Competition. Our ultimate goal is to win the competition, but more than that, to help lay the groundwork for this innovative new transportation technology. All members of this organization are committed to challenging themselves to achieve the goal of making a successful, competition-worthy pod.

Membership Requirements:
At least 75% of our members must be UW-Madison students. In accordance with Regent Policy 30-06, “Policy on Recognition of Student Organizations,” BadgerLoop as a student organization extends membership and all membership privileges, including voting and eligibility to hold office, to all students without regard to age, ethnicity, gender (except as otherwise permitted by Title IX of the Education Amendments of 1972), disability, color, national origin, religion, sexual orientation or veteran status, except those student organizations that select their members on the basis of commitment to a set of beliefs (e.g., religious or political beliefs) may limit membership and leadership positions in the organization to students who affirm that they support the organization’s goals and agree with its beliefs. Any full or part-time student may join BadgerLoop regardless of major.
